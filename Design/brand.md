# ReMastera Brand Guidelines

This document details the visual identity, brand voice, mascot character, and typographic guidelines for ReMastera.

## Brand Identity and Emotion

ReMastera is a premium, local-first video remastering studio. The name combines "Remaster" (restoring and refining media) with the ending "-a" to establish a distinct, memorable brand.

The brand should evoke:

- Nostalgia: Faded memories and family footage being treated with care.
- Transformation: Blur and shadow noise yielding to clean, detailed Masters.
- Trust: Absolute privacy, processing offline on the local Mac.
- Cinema: Warm light, film grain, soft roll-offs, and rich contrast.

## Typographic Direction

ReMastera implements a clean, Swiss International Typographic layout structure. The following font family selections are recommended:

- **Brand Header / Accent**: Bebas Neue (for large cinematic headers).
- **Body Text**: DM Sans (for readable labels, descriptions, and lists).
- **Metadata / Logs / Estimates**: DM Mono (for file sizes, bitrates, and terminal progress indicators).

Futuristic or overly detailed sci-fi typefaces should be avoided to preserve a timeless, creative studio aesthetic.

## Color Palette

The color system is dark-mode first, utilizing a cinematic, warm color range:

- **Deep Charcoal**: Standard application background for a professional studio look.
- **Warm Gold / Amber**: Accent color for active progress, highlights, and successful complete states.
- **Muted Teal / Cyan**: Shadow bias for cinematic contrast.
- **Clean Off-White**: Primary text color for high contrast readability.

Neon or flashy colors must not be used.

## Mascot Character: Remy the Lightkeeper

To help creators navigate technical settings, ReMastera introduces **Remy the Lightkeeper**. 

Remy is a tiny, helpful archivist who carries a warm, frame-shaped lantern. Remy is quirky, honest, and protective of user privacy.

### Remy Messages

- "I will keep this local. No files will be uploaded."
- "This export might get heavy. Do you want a smaller HEVC version?"
- "Done. Your footage has a new master."
- "That setting creates archival files. Beautiful, but large."

Remy appears in empty states, onboarding slides, and progress screens. Remy must be used sparingly to maintain the professional utility character of the application.
